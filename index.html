ABOUT

Future Software Engineer | Mountain Enthusiast | Involved Citizen


I enjoy problem solving, learning, and idea development. I am a creative person who loves to create new projects and engage with others. I have been self-learning in Web Development over the last two years and this September I completed my first year in Computing Science at the Thompson River University.

During the summer months I will be taking a Web Development Bootcamp with Brainstation.

Previously to entering school last September I lived in France for four years and fell in love with Serre Chevalier. To develop my skills in programming and enjoy the area around me I created Bike Serre Chevalier, a bike web application because I found that the bike trails around Serre Chevalier were poorly communicated. I built Bike Serre Chevalier with node.js and QGIS. I recreated Bike Serre Chevalier into Bike Briançon because I wanted to rebrand it, host it from my Github, and work on learning new languages. Bike Briançon was built using HTML, Bootstrap, QGIS, and Javascript. I put myself out in the bike community, speaking with biking guides, collecting trail data which I rode, and data scraping trail data from existing sites.

I found my artistic side with Briançon Fantômes by creating a french theatre street show in the summer of 2020, while I was living in Briançon, France. It evolved into an audio tour (in French and English).

I have also participated in two Hackathons. The projects are linked below.
