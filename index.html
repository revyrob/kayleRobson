ABOUT

Software Engineer | Mountain Enthusiast | Involved Citizen


I am a projected oriented person who enjoys a challenge. In the last two years I started programming and found it suits my style of continual learning and ability to touch upon many different sectors. I have launched a handful of websites and worked remotely on Hackathons. I am currently completing a Web Development Bootcamp. My past work experiences show that I am adaptable and dynamic.

I am currently completing a Web Development Bootcamp with Brainstation.

My journey towards Software Engineering began seven years ago. I knew I needed a change and I was keen to study Geographic Information Systems. Yet, my plans didn’t move in a straight line since we sold a business and had two more children. As I was waiting to start the program a friend suggested I build a project in GIS. While building my first project VTT Serre Che, I discovered computer programming. I enjoyed how I was able to create and build my ideas with code. VTT Serre Che later transformed into Bike Briancon. At the time I was living in France and was passionate about the town I was living in and created an Audio Theater Tour Briancon Fantomes. From there I participated in Hackathons, helped friends with their websites, built websites for my family, and progressed using online courses. This last year I finished my first year of a Computing Science Degree. The program was online and self-study, which meant no student interaction. It gave me a great base of understanding of how computers work, Python, Java, C, C#, and discrete mathematics. Yet the course itself was lacking in materials and interactions. I networked through some Canadian Computer Science students and I found a bootcamp student whom I met for dinner and asked him questions about his own journey. That brings me to where I am now!
